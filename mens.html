Understanding Menstrual Irregularities

Menstrual irregularities refer to any changes in the regularity, frequency, or duration of menstrual periods. While it is common for some women to experience occasional irregular periods, persistent or severe changes in menstrual patterns may indicate an underlying medical condition that requires evaluation and treatment.

A menstrual cycle that varies in length, duration, or flow can indicate an underlying health issue or hormonal imbalance. In this blog post, we'll discuss the types of menstrual irregularities, their causes, and when to see an OBGYN for evaluation and treatment.

Types of Menstrual Irregularities

1. Amenorrhea: This is the absence of menstruation for three or more months in women who have previously had regular menstrual cycles.
2. Oligomenorrhea: This is a menstrual cycle that occurs more than 35 days apart.
3. Menorrhagia: This is heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding that lasts more than seven days.
4. Metrorrhagia: This is bleeding between periods, often referred to as spotting.
5. Dysmenorrhea: This is severe menstrual cramping that interferes with daily activities.

Causes of Menstrual Irregularities

1. Hormonal imbalances: Hormonal imbalances, such as polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), thyroid disorders, and adrenal gland disorders, can cause menstrual irregularities.
2. Medications: Certain medications, such as hormonal contraceptives and blood thinners, can cause menstrual irregularities.
3.Stress: Chronic stress can disrupt the hormonal balance in the body and cause menstrual irregularities.
4.Weight changes: Rapid weight gain or loss can cause menstrual irregularities.
5.Medical conditions: Certain medical conditions, such as uterine fibroids, endometriosis, and pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), can cause menstrual irregularities.

When to See an OBGYN/h3>

If you experience any of the following menstrual irregularities, you should see your OBGYN:

1. Absence of menstruation for three or more months
2. Menstrual bleeding that lasts more than seven days
3. Heavy menstrual bleeding that requires changing pads or tampons every hour
4.Severe menstrual cramping that interferes with daily activities
5.Bleeding between periods
