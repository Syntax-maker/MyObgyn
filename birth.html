Birth Control Options: Which One Is Right For You?

When it comes to birth control, there are many options available, making it challenging to decide which one is right for you. In this blog post, we'll discuss the most common birth control options, their effectiveness, and what to consider when choosing the right one for you.

Seriously, though – the medical establishment is only beginning to dig in and take a closer look at the seriousness of the changes that take place – and how they can affect women in the long term.

Birth Control Methods/Options

1. Hormonal Methods Hormonal methods of birth control use synthetic hormones to prevent ovulation. These methods include:
Birth control pills: Taken daily to prevent pregnancy, these pills contain estrogen and progestin hormones.
Birth control patch: A small patch placed on the skin that releases hormones to prevent pregnancy.
Vaginal ring: A small ring placed in the vagina that releases hormones to prevent pregnancy.

2. Barrier methods: Barrier methods of birth control prevent pregnancy by creating a physical barrier between the sperm and the egg. These include condoms (male and female), diaphragms, and cervical caps. Barrier methods are typically used in combination with spermicidal gels or creams.


3. Attend to your perineum: If you had a vaginal delivery, your perineum (the area between your vagina and anus) may be sore or swollen. Use ice packs or sitz baths to help ease the discomfort. You may also need to use perineal pads or disposable underwear for the first few days after birth.


4. Intrauterine devices (IUDs): An IUD is a small, T-shaped device that is inserted into the uterus by a healthcare provider. IUDs can be hormonal (releasing progestin) or non-hormonal (made of copper). They work by preventing fertilization and implantation of a fertilized egg.


5. Sterilization: Sterilization is a permanent form of birth control that involves surgically blocking or sealing the fallopian tubes to prevent the sperm from reaching the egg. This can be done through tubal ligation (for women) or vasectomy (for men).


6. Fertility awareness methods: Fertility awareness methods involve tracking ovulation and avoiding sex during the fertile period. This can be done through methods such as tracking basal body temperature, cervical mucus, or using ovulation prediction kits.
